  \[

  v_1=\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix},\
  v_2=\begin{bmatrix}2\\-1\end{bmatrix}.
  \]
  
  The linear map \(L:\mathbb{R}^3\to\mathbb{R}^2\) is

  ---
- ### (1) Use L(x) on E to generate a new basis G (5 pts)

  \(\det[L(u_1)\ L(u_2)]=(-2)(-1)-2(-1)=4\neq0,\)

  \[
  G=(g_1,g_2)=(L(u_1),L(u_2))=

  \]

- ### (2) Estimate P by least squares from data (10 pts)
  
  | Xw | Yw | Xr | Yr |
  |----|----|----|----|
  | 2 | 3 | 2 | 2 |
  | 4 | 5 | 3 | 5 |
  | 3 | 2 | 3 | 1 |
  
  Model  
  \[
  \begin{bmatrix}X_R\\Y_R\end{bmatrix}
  =
  \begin{bmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{bmatrix}
  \begin{bmatrix}X_W\\Y_W\end{bmatrix}.
  \]
  
  Stack equations:  
  \[
  A=
  \begin{bmatrix}
  2&3&0&0\\
  0&0&2&3\\
  4&5&0&0\\
  0&0&4&5\\
  3&2&0&0\\
  0&0&3&2
  \end{bmatrix},\quad
  \theta=\begin{bmatrix}a\\b\\c\\d\end{bmatrix},\quad
  b=\begin{bmatrix}2\\2\\3\\5\\3\\1\end{bmatrix}.
  \]
  
  Least-squares solution:
  \[
  \theta=(A^T A)^{-1}A^T b
  \Rightarrow
  a=\tfrac{43}{39},\
  b=-\tfrac{17}{78},\
  c=-\tfrac{5}{13},\
  d=\tfrac{31}{26}.
  \]
  Hence  
  \[
  P=
  \begin{bmatrix}

  \end{bmatrix}.
